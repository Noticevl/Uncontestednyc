Uncontested vs. Contested

Uncontested means there’s full agreement on money, parenting, and the decision to divorce; the other spouse cooperates or defaults. Contested means there’s a dispute on one or more issues or cooperation is unlikely. We focus on uncontested matters; if yours becomes contested, we’ll outline options and referrals.
