Residency for NYC divorces (DRL §230)

You must satisfy one of the residency paths in New York’s Domestic Relations Law §230. Common paths: two years in NY before filing; or one year with a NY tie (married in NY, marital residence in NY, or grounds in NY). See the statute text at the NY Legislature or annotated sources.
Two‑year path
Either spouse has lived in New York for the two years immediately before filing.
One‑year + NY tie
At least one spouse for one year, plus a tie: marriage in NY, marital residence in NY, or grounds in NY.
References: DRL §230 (Legislature) and annotated text.
