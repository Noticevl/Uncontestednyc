Eligibility
New York offers several residency paths. Common examples include one spouse living in NY for two continuous years before filing; or one year in NY with the marriage taking place here or the cause arising here. We’ll map your facts to a qualifying path and confirm documentation at intake.
Timeline
A typical flow is: intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many matters complete in 8–16 weeks after signatures, but timing varies by borough workloads. You’ll see a realistic range the day we onboard you.
Cost
Your $5,000 plan includes attorney time, standard NYC filing fees, and routine local service. If your case needs outside steps (Hague service, publication, translation), we’ll show the out‑of‑pocket amount before you choose it—no surprises.
Forms
You’ll only see what’s needed: Summons with Notice or Verified Complaint, Sworn Statements, Affidavit of Defendant or default stack, Note of Issue equivalents, Child Support forms if applicable, and the proposed Judgment. We organize each packet so clerks can approve it without back‑and‑forth.
Service
When both spouses cooperate, we use the simplest lawful service. Otherwise we follow CPLR/DRL‑compliant personal service or court‑approved alternatives if necessary.
E‑filing
We use NYSCEF wherever accepted and EDDS where directed. You’ll receive confirmations and a tidy record of everything submitted.
Parenting & custody
If you have children, your plan must be workable. We write plainly: where the child lives, the parenting time schedule, holidays, travel, decision‑making, and how you’ll communicate about the child.
Child support
The CSSA formula starts with combined parental income and applies percentages based on number of children. We run the math with caps and add‑ons and include the worksheets for review.
Property & debt
List the major items (home, retirement, car, credit cards) and write simple terms for who keeps what and how to transfer or pay. Clear details reduce clerk questions.
Name change
If you’d like a former name restored, we add it to the pleadings and Judgment so you have a single, permanent document.
After the divorce
Close the loop: update beneficiaries, titles, and accounts; handle ODR or fines if needed; download your final documents for records.
Common mistakes
Vague parenting terms, missing service proof, unreadable scans, and skipping required forms. We prevent these with checklists and two rounds of review.
