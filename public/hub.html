NYC Divorce Hub

Plain‑English guidance for uncontested divorces in NYC. Use the tabs to browse topics at your pace.

Eligibility

New York has several residency paths under DRL §230. Example: one spouse living in NY for two continuous years before filing; or one spouse for a year with New York ties (marriage here or cause arising here). We confirm the path at intake.

Timeline

Intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many matters complete in about 8–16 weeks after signatures, depending on borough workload.

Settlement terms

Your agreement should be clear, workable, and specific: property, debts, parenting if any, and support. We use clerk‑friendly formatting so it’s accepted the first time.

Name change

Restoration can be included in the pleadings and the Judgment so you have a single, permanent record.

Forms

You’ll only see what’s needed: Summons with Notice or Verified Complaint, Sworn Statements, Acknowledgments and defaults if applicable, and the Judgment packet. We assemble it for easy review and filing.

Cost

$5,000 all‑in covers attorney time, standard filing fees, and routine service. Any unusual expenses are quoted separately before you choose them.

Parenting plan

Be clear about schedules, holidays, decision‑making, communication, and travel. Clarity now prevents friction later and helps the clerk approve your papers.

Child support

New York uses the CSSA formula with income caps and add‑ons (child care, health care, activities). We run the numbers and include the required worksheets so review is smooth.

Service

When spouses cooperate, we use the simplest lawful service with clear acknowledgments. Otherwise, we arrange compliant personal service or court‑approved alternatives.

E‑filing

We use NYSCEF when accepted and EDDS when directed. You get receipts and a neat archive of submissions.

After the divorce

We close the loop: final copies, name‑change follow‑through, title/beneficiary updates, and practical to‑dos.

Post‑judgment help

Simple clarifications, certified copies, and how to request a modification if circumstances change later.
