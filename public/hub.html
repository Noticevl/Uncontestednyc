Glossary

Eligibility

Multiple residency paths under DRL §230—e.g., one spouse in NY for 2 years; or 1 year with NY ties. We’ll confirm which applies.

Timeline

Intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many finish 8–16 weeks after signatures.

Settlement terms

Keep it concrete and workable: property, debts, and (if applicable) parenting terms and support.

Name change

Restoration in the Judgment so it’s one permanent document—no second petition.

Digital signing

We use e‑sign and RON where acceptable; otherwise we help arrange notarization.

Common mistakes

Vague parenting language, missing service proof, and unreadable scans. We prevent these with checklists and two revision rounds.

Parenting & custody

Workable terms: residence, time schedule, holidays, decision‑making, travel, communication.

Child support

CSSA math with combined income and add‑ons. We’ll run the worksheet and discuss deviations where appropriate.

E‑filing

NYSCEF wherever accepted; EDDS where directed. You receive confirmations and a tidy record.

Service

Simplest lawful method when spouses cooperate; otherwise CPLR‑compliant personal service or court‑approved alternatives.

Wrap‑up tasks

Beneficiaries, titles, and accounts; obtain certified copies if needed; archive your PDFs.

Key terms

Uncontested: both spouses agree on all terms. CSSA: NY’s child support math. NYSCEF: e‑filing portal. EDDS: document delivery system.
