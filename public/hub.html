NYC Divorce Hub

Eligibility

NY residency paths under DRL §230; we confirm which applies based on your facts.

Timeline

Intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many matters finish 8–16 weeks after signatures.

Forms

Summons with Notice or Verified Complaint, affidavits, service proof, settlement terms, and judgment papers.

Costs

$5,000 flat includes standard court fees and routine service. Unusual costs are cleared with you first.

Parenting plan

Plain terms: schedule, holidays, travel, decision‑making, and communication.

Child support

We run CSSA math with caps/add‑ons; we include the worksheets required for clerk review.

Service

Simplest lawful method first; otherwise CPLR‑compliant alternatives.

E‑filing

We use NYSCEF wherever accepted and EDDS when directed.

After the divorce

Update beneficiaries, titles, and accounts; download and store final documents.

Name change

If requested, the Judgment includes restoration, so you can update IDs in one pass.

Queens / Bronx / Staten Island

Affidavit of Defendant

A sworn statement, usually signed by the non‑filing spouse in uncontested cases, confirming service and agreeing to proceed without further notice.

Affidavit of Service

Proof that the divorce papers were served in a legally acceptable way (e.g., personal delivery under CPLR 308).

Appearance & Waiver

A signed document from the defendant spouse acknowledging the case and waiving further formal notice.

CPLR 308

New York rule describing legally acceptable methods of serving papers (personal, substitute, nail‑and‑mail, etc.).

CSSA (Child Support Standards Act)

New York’s child support formula. Applies a percentage to combined parental income, with caps and add‑ons.

Default Divorce

When the defendant spouse does not appear or sign. The court can grant a judgment after proper service and time has passed.

DRL §170

New York Domestic Relations Law §170 lists grounds for divorce (including irretrievable breakdown for at least 6 months).

DRL §236(B)

The main New York statute governing equitable distribution, maintenance, and other financial issues in divorce.

EDDS

Electronic Document Delivery System—used to transmit documents to courts that do not accept e‑filing for the specific document.

Equitable Distribution

New York’s approach to dividing marital property fairly (not strictly 50/50).

Findings & Judgment

The final order(s) that end the marriage and set out the terms approved by the court.

Index Number

The case number assigned when the action is commenced in Supreme Court.

Maintenance

Spousal support (sometimes called alimony). Can be durational or non‑durational, and may follow guideline ranges.

NYSCEF

New York State Courts Electronic Filing system. Many Supreme Courts accept e‑filing for uncontested divorces.

Note of Issue

A filing that places the case on the court’s calendar in certain contexts; for uncontested divorces, the equivalent fee is typically included in the all‑in price.

Parenting Plan

Terms covering decision‑making, schedule, holidays, travel, and communication for children.

Process Server

A person authorized to serve legal documents according to CPLR requirements.

Pro Se

Representing yourself without an attorney.

RJI (Request for Judicial Intervention)

A request to assign a judge. Often not required for straightforward uncontested divorces.

RON (Remote Online Notarization)

New York permits RON under specific rules; we’ll advise when practical and accepted.

Settlement Agreement

A written agreement resolving property, support, and parenting terms; incorporated into the Judgment.

Service Window

The time period after filing when the defendant must be served.

Stipulation of Discontinuance

A filing to end a case if the parties reconcile before judgment.

Sworn Statement — Barriers to Remarriage

A statement confirming religious barriers have been removed when applicable (e.g., get in Jewish divorces).

Uncontested Divorce

Both spouses agree on all issues; the court reviews papers without a trial.
