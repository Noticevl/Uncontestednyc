Basics Parenting & Support Filing & Service After Judgment Borough Guides Glossary

Eligibility

Multiple residency paths under DRL §230—e.g., one spouse in NY for 2 years; or 1 year with NY ties or marriage here. We’ll confirm which applies.

Timeline

Intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many finish 8–16 weeks after signatures; varies by borough workload.

Settlement terms

Plain‑English, workable provisions for parenting time, support, and division of property/debt, kept practical for clerk review.

Parenting plan

Where children live, how time is shared, holidays, travel, decision‑making, and communication. Keep it clear and enforceable.

Child support

CSSA uses combined income and percentages. We walk caps/add‑ons and include the worksheets needed for review.

Health & expenses

Health insurance, unreimbursed medicals, childcare, and activities—documented so everyone knows what’s expected.

Service

When both spouses cooperate, we use the simplest lawful service. Otherwise CPLR/DRL‑compliant personal service or court‑approved alternatives.

E‑filing / EDDS

NYSCEF where accepted and EDDS where directed. You receive receipts and a tidy record of everything submitted.

Forms

Summons with Notice or Verified Complaint, Sworn Statements, Affidavit of Defendant or default stack, and Judgment packet.

Name change

Include a restoration now for a single, permanent record.

After the divorce

Close out beneficiaries, titles, and accounts; we provide a final PDF set for your records.

Certified copies

We’ll explain how to obtain certified copies from the County Clerk after entry of Judgment.

NYSCEF

New York State Courts Electronic Filing—accepted in many matrimonial parts for motion practice; EDDS often used for document delivery.
