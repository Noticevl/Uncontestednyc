Eligibility

DRL §230 offers several residency paths—e.g., one spouse in NY for two years; or one year with NY ties. We’ll confirm which applies and collect the proof the clerk expects.

Timeline

Intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many matters complete 8–16 weeks after signatures; exact timing depends on the borough.

Settlement terms

We write in plain English but follow court conventions so nothing is ambiguous. Clear language reduces questions and keeps things moving.

Name change

If you want a former name restored, we place it in the pleadings and the Judgment so you have one permanent document for agencies.

Parenting plan

Include residence, parenting time, holidays, travel, decision‑making, and communication. Specific but workable terms help clerks approve without edits.

Child support

We apply CSSA percentages using combined parental income, current caps, and add‑ons, and include the required worksheets to match the math.

E‑filing

We use NYSCEF where accepted and EDDS where directed. You receive stamped confirmations and a tidy record of everything submitted.

Service

When both spouses cooperate we use the simplest lawful service. Otherwise we follow CPLR/DRL‑compliant personal service or court‑approved alternatives.

Close‑out

After entry: update beneficiaries and titles, order certified copies if needed, and store your final documents in a secure archive.

Certified copies

We’ll show where and how to order certified copies from the county clerk if an agency asks for one.

Glossary A–M

Glossary N–Z

Checklist — before drafting

Names as on IDs • Addresses • Wedding date/place • Agreement outline • Child details (if any). Ready to go = faster drafting.

Checklist — filing day

Signed pages • Notarizations • Filing fee receipt • Confirmed service plan.
