NYC Divorce Hub

Plain‑English guidance for uncontested divorces in NYC. Use the tabs to browse topics without opening new pages.

Eligibility Multiple residency paths under Domestic Relations Law §230—e.g., one spouse in NY for two years; or one year with NY ties. We’ll confirm which applies.
Timeline Intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many finish 8–16 weeks after signatures; exact timing varies by borough workload.
Settlement terms We translate agreements into clerk‑friendly language. The clearer your terms, the smoother the review.
Name change If you want a former name restored, we add it to the pleadings and the Judgment so you have a single, permanent document.
Property & debt List the major items (home, retirement, vehicles, credit cards) and say who keeps what and whether any buy‑out or transfer is owed.
Taxes & benefits Coordinate filing status, dependency claims, and benefits changes to avoid surprises next April.
Parenting plan We focus on clarity: where the child lives, school and healthcare decisions, holiday schedule, travel notices, and how you’ll communicate.
Child support New York’s CSSA formula uses combined parental income and a percentage based on the number of children. We show the math with current caps and add‑ons.
Health & extras Spell out coverage, extracurriculars, daycare, and how reimbursements work (amounts, timing, receipts).
Judicial review Parenting provisions must be workable and in the child’s best interests. We draft to pass clerk and judge review.
Forms Summons with Notice or Verified Complaint, Sworn Statements, Note of Issue equivalents, Child Support forms if applicable, and the proposed Judgment.
Service When both spouses cooperate, we use the simplest lawful service. Otherwise CPLR/DRL‑compliant process service or court‑approved alternatives.
E‑filing We use NYSCEF whenever accepted and EDDS where directed. You receive confirmations and a tidy record of all submissions.
Special cases International service, publication, and translation are handled when needed—with costs and timing shown up front.
Wrap‑up Update beneficiaries and titles, close out joint accounts, and save your final documents securely.
Certified copies If you need certified copies, we’ll outline how to order them and what to bring.
Post‑judgment changes Most uncontested matters finish here. If you later need a modification, ask about a quick options call.
Records We keep a tidy file: docket confirmations, service proofs, and the final Judgment.
Manhattan (New York County) NYSCEF widely used; clerk workloads fluctuate. We format to current preferences.
Brooklyn (Kings County) Typical timing is similar to Manhattan; EDDS may appear for specific filings.
Queens Expect clear service proof and consistent nomenclature across forms.
Bronx Allow buffer for review queues; we sequence to minimize stops and starts.
Staten Island (Richmond) Often the most straightforward when papers are clean; we keep it that way.
NYSCEF New York State Courts Electronic Filing system.
EDDS Electronic Document Delivery System used by NY courts where NYSCEF isn’t available for a filing type.
Judgment The final court order ending the marriage.
Service The formal delivery of legal papers that proves your spouse received them.
