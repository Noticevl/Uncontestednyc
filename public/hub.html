NYC Divorce Hub

This library explains the uncontested process in NYC without legal jargon. Open a topic below to read in place.

Eligibility

New York offers several residency paths. We map your facts to a qualifying path and confirm documentation at intake.

Timeline

Typical flow: intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many matters complete in 8–16 weeks after signatures; borough workload drives speed.

Cost

The $5,000 plan includes attorney time, standard filing fees, and routine personal service. Outside items are quoted plainly.

Forms

You’ll see only what’s needed: Summons with Notice or Verified Complaint, affidavits, child support add‑ons when applicable, service proofs, and Judgment papers.

Service

We use the simplest lawful service. Where both spouses cooperate, acknowledgement of service saves time; otherwise, CPLR/DRL‑compliant methods.

E‑filing

We use NYSCEF where accepted and EDDS where directed. You receive confirmations and a tidy record.

Parenting & custody

Plans must be workable—residence, schedule, holidays, travel, decision‑making, communication.

Child support

CSSA percentages are applied to combined income with current caps and add‑ons. We run the worksheet and include it.

Property & debt

We list items concretely and show how ownership or payoff will change hands—fewer clerk questions.

Name change

Restore a former name in the Judgment so it’s automatic.

After the divorce

Update beneficiaries, titles, and accounts. Keep your Judgment certified copies in a safe place.

Common mistakes

Unclear parenting terms, missing service proof, unreadable scans, and skipping required forms. We avoid these with checklists and two revision rounds.

Ready-to-print intake and judgment checklists are in the printables folder.

Borough guides

Local filing notes for Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island.

Free resources
