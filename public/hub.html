NYC Divorce Hub

Plain‑English guidance for uncontested divorces in NYC. Use the tabs to browse core topics—kept tidy on a single page.

Eligibility

Multiple residency paths under DRL §230—e.g., one spouse in NY for 2 years; or 1 year with NY ties. We’ll confirm which applies.

Timeline

Intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many finish 8–16 weeks after signatures (borough workload varies).

Settlement terms

Keep it specific and workable: property, accounts, debts, vehicles, and retirement. Focus on “who keeps what” and “how to transfer.”

Name change

We can include a restoration of a former name directly in the Judgment so it’s on one court record.

Notarization

NY accepts traditional or remote online notarization (RON) for many forms when executed properly.

Court fees

Your flat fee includes standard NYC court fees. If an unusual step is required, we show the outside cost before you decide.

Parenting plan

Clarity beats vagueness: where the child lives, parenting time schedule, holidays, travel, decision‑making, and communication.

Child support

New York’s CSSA formula starts with combined income and applies set percentages. We run the math with current caps and add‑ons.

Add‑ons

Health insurance, uninsured medical, childcare, extracurriculars—allocate clearly to avoid disputes later.

E‑filing

We use NYSCEF when accepted and EDDS when directed. You’ll receive confirmations and a tidy record of everything submitted.

Service

If both spouses cooperate, we use the simplest lawful service. Otherwise, we follow CPLR/DRL‑compliant personal service or court‑approved alternatives.

Signatures

We coordinate notarization and provide exact signing instructions so forms pass clerk review the first time.

Certified copies

We’ll help you get certified Judgment copies if you need them for agencies or financial institutions.

Account updates

Update beneficiaries and titles on accounts, real estate, and vehicles; store final documents in one place.

Post‑judgment tweaks

If something small needs adjusting later, we’ll talk through options and the quickest path.

Manhattan (NY County)

Supreme Court filing via NYSCEF for most uncontested matters; plan for clerk review queues.

Brooklyn (Kings)

EDDS may be directed for certain submissions. We’ll follow the current local instructions.

Queens / Bronx / Staten Island

Process varies slightly by borough; we track any local quirks so your package is accepted the first time.

NYSCEF

New York State Courts Electronic Filing system used to e‑file documents in many courts.

EDDS

Electronic Document Delivery System—used where full e‑filing isn’t available or when a court requests it.

CSSA

Child Support Standards Act, the statute that sets the formula used by NY for support.
