NYC Divorce Hub

Plain‑English guidance for uncontested divorces in NYC.

Eligibility

New York offers several residency paths under DRL §230, including: (1) either spouse has lived in NY for two years; (2) either spouse has lived in NY for one year and the marriage took place in NY or the cause occurred in NY; or (3) both parties are residents at filing and the cause occurred in NY. We confirm the path at intake.

Timeline

Typical flow: intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many matters complete in 8–16 weeks after signatures, but timing varies by borough workload.

Forms

You’ll see only what’s needed—Summons/Complaint or Verified Complaint, Sworn Statements, Acknowledgment of Service or equivalent, Child Support forms when applicable, and the Judgment package.

Costs

Your flat $5,000 covers attorney time, standard NY filing fees, and routine local service. We show any non‑standard costs before you choose them.

Parenting plans

Keep it workable: where the child lives, parenting time schedule, holidays, travel, decision‑making, and how you’ll communicate.

Child support

New York’s CSSA applies a percentage to combined parental income with caps and add‑ons. We run the math and include the worksheets for a clean review.

Health & add‑ons

Health insurance, unreimbursed medical, childcare, and extracurriculars are commonly addressed so there are no surprises later.

Special cases

Relocation clauses or flexible schedules can be written plainly. We keep the terms realistic so the clerk accepts them.

E‑filing

We use NYSCEF whenever accepted and EDDS where directed. You’ll receive confirmations and a tidy record of everything submitted.

Service

When both spouses cooperate, we use the simplest lawful service. Otherwise we follow CPLR/DRL‑compliant personal service or court‑approved alternatives if needed.

Notary & signing

We accommodate RON or in‑person notarization. You’ll get clear signing instructions with every packet.

After filing

We monitor clerk notes and correct minor issues before they cause delay. Two rounds of revisions are included.

Final Judgment

Once Judgment enters, you’ll receive certified copies and a complete digital archive for your records.

Clean wrap‑up

We guide name‑change notices and beneficiary/account updates so everything matches the Judgment.
