NYC Divorce Hub

Eligibility

Multiple residency paths under DRL §230—e.g., one spouse in NY for 2 years; or 1 year with NY ties. We’ll confirm which applies.

Timeline

Intake → drafting → signatures → filing → clerk review → Judgment. Many finish 8–16 weeks after signatures, depending on borough workload.

Terms to include

Plain terms on property, accounts, debts, parenting (if any), and name change. Fewer gaps = fewer clerk questions.

Name change

If you want a former name restored, we place it in the pleadings and the Judgment so you have one permanent document.

Parenting & custody

Specify where the child lives, a workable parenting‑time schedule, travel/holiday rules, and how you’ll decide new issues.

Child support

NY’s CSSA applies percentages to combined parental income with caps and add‑ons. We’ll run the math and include the worksheets.

Add‑ons

Health insurance, uncovered medical, and childcare are split by percentage or a clear fixed method—whatever fits your facts.

E‑filing

We use NYSCEF whenever accepted; EDDS where directed. You get confirmations and a tidy record of everything submitted.

Service

If both spouses cooperate, we use the simplest lawful service. Else we follow CPLR/DRL methods or court‑approved alternatives.

Judgment packet

We compile clerk‑friendly packets with signatures, notary blocks, and exhibits in the order your borough prefers.

After the divorce

Update beneficiaries, titles, and accounts; download certified copies for your records; and store a clean digital set.

Post‑judgment

Need a correction or simple enforcement? We’ll explain options and costs before we move.
