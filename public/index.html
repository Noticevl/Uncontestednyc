FAQ — clear answers before you decide

Eligibility & Grounds

What does “truly uncontested” mean?

Both spouses agree on all issues (property, support, parenting) and the other spouse cooperates or will default.

Do I qualify under DRL §230 (residency)?

You typically qualify if you lived in NY for 2 years, or 1 year with a NY tie (married in NY, marital residence in NY, or grounds in NY).

What grounds do you use?

Usually DRL §170(7) — irretrievable breakdown for at least six months — unless another ground is preferable.

Can we file if my spouse is out of state?

Yes; service outside NYC may change logistics but uncontested cases usually proceed smoothly.

Money & Fees

What’s not included?

Service & Missing Spouse

What if I don’t know where my spouse lives?

We can explore alternate service (e.g., publication) with the court; these costs are not in the flat fee.

Can you arrange service outside NYC?

Yes — non‑standard costs may apply; we’ll quote before proceeding.

Process & Filing

Do I have to appear in court?

Most uncontested cases are decided on papers without an appearance.

Do you e‑file?

Yes. We use NYSCEF where available or EDDS/document delivery where e‑filing isn’t permitted.

What if a judge asks questions?

We respond in writing or by conference if requested—guided every step.

Can you work with my mediator’s agreement?

Yes — we review and convert mediated terms into court‑ready documents.

Children & Support

Can we include a parenting plan?

Yes — we’ll incorporate parenting time and decision‑making details into your agreement.

How is child support handled?

We include Child Support Standards Act language; if you vary, we include the required explanations.

Paperwork & After Judgment

Do you help with name changes?

Yes — we can include name change language in the judgment.

How do I get certified copies?

We’ll guide you; extra certified copies are typically about $10 each.

What should I do after the divorce?

Update IDs, beneficiaries, titles, and follow support/parenting steps in your post‑judgment checklist.
