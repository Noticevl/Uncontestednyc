Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?

Usually no for straightforward uncontested matters. Most are completed through e‑filing and mail with no in‑person appearance.

How long does it take?

Many cases complete in 8–16 weeks after signatures and filing, subject to the borough’s review timelines.

What exactly does the $5,000 include?

Attorney time from intake to Judgment, standard NYC filing fees, and routine local service. Any non‑routine costs are shown up front for approval.

Are there refunds?

Yes. Staged refunds: Stage 1 before drafting starts; Stage 2 before filing/service; Stage 3 before judgment work begins.

Do both of us have to live in New York?

No. New York’s residency statute offers several qualifying paths. We’ll map your facts to one at intake.

What counts as “uncontested”?

Both spouses agree on all divorce terms and neither plans to file responses contesting relief.

Can we e‑file?

We use NYSCEF wherever permitted and EDDS when directed.

What if we have children?

We draft clear parenting terms—residence, time schedule, holidays, travel, decision‑making, and communication.

How is child support calculated?

New York applies the CSSA formula to combined income with statutory percentages and caps. We run the worksheet with current figures.

Can I keep my home address private?

Yes. We can use a safe mailing address and keep your apartment number off public papers where possible.

Can I restore my former name?

Yes—add it now so Judgment restores it automatically.

What if my spouse lives out of state or country?

We’ll use lawful service for that location; if publication is required, we’ll show the extra cost before proceeding.

Do we need a settlement agreement?

Many couples do; some use affidavits and proposed Judgment terms. We’ll suggest the simplest compliant path.

Is mediation required?

No, not for uncontested filings. If you need help finishing terms, we can pause for mediation and resume.

Do I need to be a NYC resident?

Not strictly—see residency paths. NYC filing venue is based on residence or where marriage occurred.

Will a judge ever call me?

Rarely. Most communication is via clerk review notes we handle for you.

How do notarizations work?

In‑person notaries or NY‑compliant remote online notarization (RON) when eligible.

Can we include custom parenting provisions?

Yes—so long as they’re workable and child‑focused. We translate goals into clear, enforceable terms.

Do we need financial disclosures?

Basic income info supports child support; full discovery is generally unnecessary in uncontested cases.

What are the filing fees?

Included in the $5,000 plan: Index Number and Note of Issue equivalents. Other rare fees are discussed up front.

Can I sign everything electronically?

We use e‑sign for drafts and affidavits when acceptable; wet ink is used where the court requires.

What if I need my Judgment fast?

We sequence drafting and filing quickly; actual clerk timing is borough‑dependent.

What if we own property together?

We’ll make the terms concrete about who keeps what and how transfers occur.

What if we already separated years ago?

That’s fine—uncontested is still possible if you both agree now.
