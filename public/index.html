Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?
Not usually. Uncontested divorces in NYC are mostly paperwork-driven. If your filings are in order and the clerk has no issues, there is typically no in-person appearance.
How long does an uncontested divorce take in NYC?
A few months is common once all signatures are complete and service is done. Timing varies by county workload and whether corrections are needed.
What exactly is included in the $5,000 flat fee?
Attorney time, standard NYC filing fees, and routine personal service within the five boroughs for a straightforward uncontested matter. If anything unusual is needed—translation, international service, or publication—we’ll quote that in writing before work begins.
Is the fee refundable?
Yes—before work is rendered at each stage. The engagement letter breaks the matter into three stages (Intake & Drafting, Filing & Service, Judgment & Closeout) and you may request a refund before the next stage starts.
Can you file via NYSCEF?
Yes. Where available, we use NYSCEF for Supreme Court e‑filing; otherwise we route through EDDS or clerk-preferred channels.
Do both spouses need New York residency?
No. New York has multiple residency paths (e.g., one spouse living in NY for two years; or one year when the marriage occurred in NY or grounds occurred in NY). We’ll confirm the best route for your facts.
What if we have children?
It can still be uncontested as long as you agree on custody, parenting time, and child support. Your agreement must include the CSSA recitals and any deviation reasons so the clerk can accept it.
Do we need a settlement agreement?
If you’ve already agreed on everything, a signed and properly acknowledged settlement agreement keeps the case smooth. Some couples proceed with a detailed stipulation of settlement instead—either way the notary acknowledgment must be correct.
How is child support calculated?
Under the CSSA, the basic obligation is a percentage of combined parental income up to the statutory cap, divided pro‑rata. Your agreement must include the required notices and any deviation statement.
Will I get a certified copy of the Judgment?
Yes. After entry, you can request certified copies from the county clerk for agencies, benefits, or name‑change updates.
Can you help if my spouse is out of state?
Often yes. If they’ll sign an acknowledgment, we may avoid formal service. If not, we’ll coordinate compliant service where they live or seek court permission for an alternative.
Can I keep my home address private on public filings?
We can discuss confidentiality tools (e.g., omission of apartment number where rules allow and use of safe contact info). We’ll follow local rules and clerk preferences.
Do you handle contested issues?
This practice is focused on uncontested matters. If disputes arise, we can discuss options: limited‑scope review, mediation referral, or litigation counsel.
What are the typical court fees?
Your flat fee covers standard NYC filing fees (index number, note of issue where applicable) under this plan. Extraordinary fees are quoted only if needed.
Can we change our names as part of the divorce?
Yes—name restoration can be included and reflected in the Judgment so agencies can update records.
Do you work in all five boroughs?
Yes—Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island. We tailor filings to each clerk’s preferences.
How do we sign documents?
We’ll provide clear instructions for notarization. Remote online notarization (RON) may be available through approved providers if appropriate.
What if we can’t find the marriage certificate?
You can order a replacement from the issuing jurisdiction. We’ll point you to the correct office and what details you need to request it.
Can I do parts of this myself?
Yes—some clients ask us to review drafts or file only. The flat fee is for full service; we can discuss limited‑scope options if you prefer.
When is the divorce final?
When the signed Judgment is entered by the clerk. We’ll let you know as soon as it’s recorded and how to get certified copies.
Will the court reject my paperwork?
Clerks can require corrections if forms or acknowledgments are off. Our goal is to prevent that with checklists and locally tuned templates.
How do we prove service?
Either through an Affidavit of Defendant (they acknowledge service and consent) or a sworn Affidavit of Service from a process server if formal service is used.
Can we e‑file everything the same way in every borough?
Not exactly. Acceptance differs across counties and by document type. We follow the local guidance for NYSCEF and EDDS to avoid delays.
What if my spouse is on active duty?
Special protections may apply. We’ll walk through SCRA considerations and any consent/appearance needs for an uncontested case.
Do you offer a quick consult?
Yes—free 15‑minute fit call to confirm eligibility and the path forward.
