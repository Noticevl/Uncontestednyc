Your path to an uncontested NYC divorce

1) Eligibility & grounds

We confirm residency under DRL §230 and use New York’s no‑fault ground — “irretrievable breakdown” for at least six months — unless another ground is preferable.

See: DRL §230 & DRL §170

2) Agreement & filings

We draft or review your settlement agreement and prepare all Supreme Court papers. Where permitted, we e‑file through NYSCEF and coordinate personal service.

See: NYSCEF info & Service requirements

3) Judgment & closure

Once the court processes your uncontested packet, a judge signs the Judgment. We provide next‑steps (name change, certified copies) and a tidy close‑out.

Timeline varies by county and court workload; many uncontested cases finish in a few months.

Uncontested eligibility checklist

Residency (DRL §230)

  • Married in NY and either spouse lived in NY for 1 year; or
  • Lived in NY as spouses and either spouse lived in NY for 1 year; or
  • Grounds arose in NY and both spouses live in NY; or
  • Either spouse lived in NY for 2 years immediately before filing.

Truly uncontested means

  • You both agree on property/debt division
  • Agreement on parenting plan & support (if kids)
  • No protective orders blocking communication
  • Spouse can be personally served (or court permits alternate service)

If disputes pop up, we can pivot to mediation or a different scope.

Frequently asked questions

What does the $5,000 cover exactly?

All legal work for a truly uncontested divorce: strategy consult, drafting/review, filings, coordination of personal service, tracking to judgment, and two revision rounds. Court fees, process server costs, QDROs, deeds, and contested issues are separate.

Is $5,000 the total cost?

No. Court filing fees are separate and set by the State (typically at least $335). You may also incur process server or certified copy fees.

How long does an uncontested divorce take?

It varies by county and caseload. Many uncontested cases finalize within a few months after filing, assuming clean paperwork and timely service.

Can we file online?

Often, yes. Many counties permit consensual e‑filing through NYSCEF. We’ll confirm availability and secure required consents.

Do we have to appear in court?

Most uncontested cases are decided on papers without appearances. If a judge requests clarification, we’ll guide you.

We have kids. Is uncontested still possible?

Yes, if you agree on custody, parenting time, and support consistent with New York law. We’ll ensure the agreement language fits court requirements.

What if I can’t locate my spouse?

Personal service is the default. If diligent efforts fail, we can move for court‑approved alternate service. Timelines will extend.

Will you negotiate with my spouse?

Light negotiation within the uncontested scope is fine. If issues become contested, we’ll discuss a new scope or mediation.

Can I change my name as part of the divorce?

Yes. We can include the name change in the Judgment and provide guidance on updating IDs and records.

Do you guarantee a result or timeline?

No lawyer can guarantee outcomes or timing. We’ll move efficiently and keep you informed, but decisions and timing rest with the court.

Is this plan right for me if we disagree on something?

Uncontested means 100% agreement on all issues. If not, we can start with mediation or a different scope.

Can you draft a QDRO or transfer our co‑op?

Those are outside the flat fee. We’ll quote add‑ons or refer trusted specialists.

Will you talk to my spouse or their lawyer?

Yes, professionally and efficiently, to finalize documents and filings.

Do you offer payment plans?

Our model is an upfront flat fee. If you need a different arrangement, ask — we’ll explore options consistent with NY rules.

Do you take cases outside NYC?

We handle matters throughout New York State. Ask about your county.

Is contacting you confidential?

Your inquiry is treated respectfully, but no attorney‑client relationship exists until we sign a written retainer. Please don’t send confidential details until then.

Do you use “specialist” or “expert” labels?

No. We focus our practice on uncontested divorce, but we don’t use unapproved specialist claims.

Can you rush my case?

We move promptly. Court timelines are outside our control, and we won’t promise unrealistic speed.

Will you retain my files?

Yes, per our file retention policy and New York rules. We’ll provide copies electronically for your records.

What should I bring to the kickoff?

ID, marriage certificate if available, any prenuptial agreements, and a bullet list of agreed terms (property, debts, parenting, support).
