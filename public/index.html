FAQ — clear answers before you decide

What does “truly uncontested” mean?
Both spouses agree on all issues (property, support, parenting) and the other spouse cooperates or will default. See Uncontested vs. contested.
Residency rules (DRL §230)
Generally, two years in NY, or one year with a NY tie (married in NY, marital residence in NY, or grounds in NY). See the Residency guide.
Grounds for divorce
Most uncontested cases use DRL §170(7), irretrievable breakdown for 6+ months. See the Grounds guide.
Do I have to appear in court?
Most uncontested matters are decided on papers. See Timeline.
What’s included in the $5,000 all‑in fee?
Attorney time + standard NY filing fees + routine NYC personal service. See Cost & what’s included.
Remote notarization?
New York permits electronic/remote notarization with specific rules. See Notarization guide.
Can I restore a prior surname in the Judgment?
Yes; the Judgment typically includes a clause authorizing resumption of a former surname. See Name change in divorce.
