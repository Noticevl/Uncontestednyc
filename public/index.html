Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?
Usually no. Most uncontested divorces in NYC are resolved on the papers without a personal appearance. If a judge schedules a brief conference for a clarification, we’ll let you know in advance and prepare you—these are rare and short.
How long does this take?
Plan on **8–16 weeks** after signatures and filing, depending on the borough’s review queue. Factors that extend timing: missing information, service delays, or court backlogs during holidays. We’ll give you a live estimate per borough and keep you posted.
What exactly does the $5,000 include?
Attorney time from intake through judgment, drafting and filing all standard uncontested forms, routine tracking and updates, and standard NYC court filing fees. If your case needs non‑routine steps (e.g., international service, certified translations, publication), we’ll explain the cost before you decide.
Are there refunds?
Yes. You can request a full refund **before** work in a stage begins. Stage 1 covers intake & drafting, Stage 2 covers filing & service, Stage 3 covers judgment processing. If we haven’t started the next stage, you can stop with no hard feelings.
Do both of us have to live in New York?
No—New York’s **residency rules** offer multiple paths. For example, if either spouse has been a NY resident for at least two years, or for one year with the marriage in NY or the cause in NY, that satisfies residency. We’ll confirm which rule fits you at intake.
What counts as “uncontested”?
You and your spouse agree on all major issues (ending the marriage, property and debts, parenting, and support). No court hearings to resolve disputes. If something pops up mid‑process, we’ll either help you resolve it or pause so you can decide next steps.
Can we e‑file?
Yes. NYC divorces generally file via **NYSCEF** (electronic filing) or EDDS for uploads where required. We do the filing for you and confirm acceptance with the clerk.
What if we have children?
Uncontested still works if you both agree on legal custody, parenting time, and child support. We’ll draft clear, concrete language that fits NYC’s practice so clerks can process it smoothly.
How is child support calculated?
New York uses the **Child Support Standards Act (CSSA)**—a percentage of combined parental income with adjustments. We provide a calculator and walk you through typical add‑ons (childcare, health insurance, unreimbursed medical).
Can I keep my home address private?
Yes. We can list a **mailing address** and keep the exact apartment off public papers when appropriate. We’ll discuss options that balance privacy and court requirements.
Can I restore my former name?
Absolutely. It’s included. The Judgment can contain a line restoring a prior surname so you can update IDs after the divorce is entered.
What if my spouse lives out of state or overseas?
We can still do uncontested. Service options include acceptance, mail with acknowledgment, or personal service by a qualified person or agent abroad. For some countries, we may need **Hague** service or diplomatic channels, which adds time and cost.
Do we need a settlement agreement?
Helpful, and often required when you’re dividing property or addressing parenting and support. We prepare a short, readable agreement that matches court needs and your real‑life plan.
What if things become contested mid‑way?
If a dispute arises, we’ll outline options: mediation, limited-scope help to document agreements, or referral for contested litigation. We keep your paperwork organized so you don’t lose the progress you’ve made.
Can we use remote notarization?
Yes, **RON** is permitted in NY with specific identity checks and recording requirements. We’ll set it up or guide you to a compliant provider if needed.
Do I need to split retirement accounts?
Only if you want to. If you do, we’ll explain **QDRO** basics and coordinate with a specialist to draft the plan‑specific order after judgment.
Will the Judgment change my taxes?
The Judgment itself doesn’t change filing status until it’s entered. Support can have tax effects; we’ll flag common issues and suggest when to consult a CPA.
Can we waive child support?
Courts require that children’s needs are met. You can deviate from guidelines with a written reason and adequate support shown. We’ll draft it in court‑accepted language.
Can one lawyer represent both spouses?
In uncontested matters, one lawyer typically represents one spouse and the other signs an **acknowledgment** of unrepresented status or uses independent counsel. We’ll do conflict checks and handle the paperwork cleanly.
Do we have to divide everything 50/50?
New York uses **equitable distribution**—fair, not necessarily equal. If you agree on a split that’s reasonable and clear, the court will usually accept it without a hearing.
Can we include a parenting plan?
Yes, and it’s recommended. Simple, specific language about schedules, exchanges, travel, and decision‑making avoids future conflict and clerk rejections.
What if my spouse won’t sign?
Uncontested requires signatures. If your spouse won’t participate, that becomes a different process (default or contested). We’ll explain options before you spend money on filings.
Can we keep our finances private?
We’ll minimize sensitive details in public filings and attach financial exhibits only when required. Agreements can summarize terms without listing every account number.
Do we need to appear together?
No. We can coordinate signatures separately and file electronically. You never need to meet in the same room if that’s not comfortable.
How do we prove residency?
Common proofs include a NY driver’s license, lease/utility bills, paystubs, or tax returns showing NY address. We’ll tell you exactly what the clerk will accept per borough.
Can we change the agreement later?
Some terms (like parenting and child support) can be modified by later stipulation or court order if circumstances change. Property division is usually final once the Judgment is entered.
Do we need a court date to finalize?
Not usually. The clerk and judge review the papers and issue the Judgment without a hearing. We’ll deliver a file‑stamped copy for your records.
How do I get certified copies?
We can request certified copies for name changes, retirement plans, or deeds. There’s a small fee per copy; we’ll handle the order if you want.
What happens to our deed or titles?
If you’re transferring real estate or vehicles, we’ll prepare deeds/DMV forms to match the settlement terms. Some filings happen after Judgment; we’ll calendar them for you.
What if I need the Judgment quickly?
Tell us at intake. We’ll propose a service plan and draft schedule aimed at the earliest realistic submission date for your borough, and we’ll push updates proactively.
