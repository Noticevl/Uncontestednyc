Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?
You usually don’t. Once your papers are signed and filed, NYC uncontested divorces are handled mostly by the clerk’s office.
How long does it take?
A few months is typical once signatures and service are complete. Each borough’s speed varies by workload.
What’s included in the $5,000 flat fee?
Attorney time, standard NYC filing fees, and routine personal service within the five boroughs. If your case needs unusual steps—like translation or international service—we’ll tell you up front and in writing.
Can I get a refund?
Yes, before work is done in each of the three stages (Intake & Drafting; Filing & Service; Judgment & Closeout).
Can we e‑file?
Yes. Where NYSCEF is available, we use it. Otherwise we submit through EDDS or clerk‑preferred channels.
Do both of us need to live in NY?
No. New York has several residency paths. We’ll pick the one that matches your facts.
What if we have kids?
You can still do an uncontested divorce if you agree on custody, parenting time, and support. We’ll include the child‑support language the court needs.
Do we need a settlement agreement?
It helps. If you’ve already agreed on everything, a signed and notarized settlement agreement keeps the process smooth.
How is child support calculated?
New York’s Child Support Standards Act applies a percentage to combined income (up to a cap) and divides it between parents based on income share.
Will I receive certified copies?
Yes, after Judgment we’ll show you how to obtain certified copies from the county clerk.
Out‑of‑state spouse?
Often fine. If they’ll sign, we can avoid formal service. If not, we coordinate proper service where they live.
Keeping my home address private?
We use safe contact details and follow local rules. We’ll discuss options before filing.
What happens if something becomes contested?
We’re focused on uncontested matters. If disputes arise, we’ll discuss mediation or litigation counsel.
Court fees included?
Standard NYC filing fees are included in the $5,000 plan.
Can I restore my former name?
Yes—include it in the Judgment so agencies can update records easily.
Do you work in all five boroughs?
Yes: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island.
How do we sign?
In person with a notary or via approved remote notarization providers when appropriate.
What if we lost our marriage certificate?
You can order a copy from the issuing office. We’ll point you to the right place and what to ask for.
Can I DIY parts?
Yes, but the flat fee is for full service. If you prefer limited help, we can discuss narrow‑scope options.
When is it final?
When the Judgment of Divorce is signed and entered by the clerk. We’ll confirm once it’s on record.
Why do courts reject papers?
Common reasons are missing notary language, wrong captions, or incomplete child‑support recitals. Our templates aim to prevent that.
How do we prove service?
Either an Affidavit of Defendant (they acknowledge and consent) or an Affidavit of Service from a process server.
Is e‑filing identical in every borough?
No. Each county has small differences. We follow the current local guidance.
Active‑duty spouse?
Special protections may apply. We’ll walk through what’s needed for an uncontested path.
Quick consult?
Yes—free 15‑minute fit call to confirm eligibility and timing.
