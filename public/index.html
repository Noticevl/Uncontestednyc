Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?
Usually no. Most NYC uncontested divorces are handled by the clerk after your papers are signed and filed. If anything changes, we’ll tell you beforehand.
How long does this take?
A few months is common once papers are signed and service is complete. The timeline depends on your borough’s clerk queue and how quickly signatures happen.
What exactly does the $5,000 include?
Attorney time, standard NYC filing fees, and routine personal service within the five boroughs. If your case needs something unusual—like translation, publication, or international service—we’ll explain the cost up front before you decide.
Are there refunds?
Yes—before work is done in each stage: (1) Intake & Drafting, (2) Filing & Service, (3) Judgment & Closeout. We keep this in writing so there’s no guessing.
What counts as “uncontested”?
You both agree to end the marriage and you agree on the terms (money, property, and—if you have kids—custody/support).
Do both of us have to live in New York?
No. New York has several residency routes. Often one spouse living in NY for the last two years works; one year may work if there are other NY connections (like marrying here).
Can we e‑file?
Yes. Many filings go through NYSCEF; some documents still use EDDS. We handle the submissions and keep PDFs neat so fixes are fast if the clerk asks.
What if we have children?
You can still do an uncontested divorce if you agree on custody, parenting time, and support. Your paperwork needs specific child‑support language—we add it so the clerk has what they need.
Do we need a settlement agreement?
It helps a lot. If you’ve agreed on everything, a signed, notarized agreement makes review smoother and avoids missing details.
How is child support calculated?
New York uses a formula based on combined income (up to a cap) and divides it between parents by income share. If you choose a different amount, your agreement must explain why and confirm the guidelines were considered.
What if my spouse lives in another state or country?
Often still fine. If they’ll sign an acknowledgment/consent, we can avoid formal service. If not, we use a process server; for international service we’ll follow the right treaty or method.
Can I keep my home address private?
We use safe contact details, and we’ll discuss privacy options that fit your situation before filing.
What if things become contested mid‑way?
We focus on uncontested matters. If disputes appear, we’ll discuss mediation or a hand‑off to litigation counsel so you’re not stuck.
Can I restore my former name?
Yes. We can include it in the Judgment so Social Security, DMV, and banks can update records without a separate case.
Do you work in all five boroughs?
Yes—Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island. Each has small quirks we plan for.
How do we sign?
You can sign before a notary in person or through approved remote notarization when appropriate. We’ll prep the notary pages so it’s straightforward.
What if I lost the marriage certificate?
You can order a replacement from the issuing office. We’ll point you to the right place and what to ask for.
Can I do part of this myself?
You can, but the flat fee is for full service. If you want limited help only, we can discuss a narrower scope so you pay for exactly what you want.
When is the divorce final?
When the Judgment of Divorce is signed and entered by the clerk. We’ll confirm entry and provide next steps for updates with agencies.
Why do courts reject papers?
Common reasons: missing notary language, inconsistent names/dates, or incomplete child‑support recitals. We use NYC‑specific checklists to prevent those hiccups.
How do we prove service?
Either your spouse signs an acknowledgment/consent or we use a process server who files an affidavit of service.
Is e‑filing the same in every borough?
Not exactly. Each county has preferences. We align with current practice so filings aren’t bounced back for format issues.
What if my spouse is on active duty?
Special protections can apply. We’ll check what’s needed for an uncontested path and handle the language accordingly.
Do you offer a quick consult?
Yes—free 15‑minute fit call to confirm eligibility and timing.
What happens after the divorce?
We’ll send a practical list: certified copies, bank/benefit updates, name changes, and how to fix clerical errors if any appear.
Can we keep our own parenting plan?
Yes, if it covers the basics. We’ll check for items the court expects (decision‑making, parenting time, support details).
How do we divide property and debt?
List who keeps what and who pays which debts, with any deadlines. Clarity now prevents trouble later. For retirement accounts, you may need a special post‑judgment order; we’ll flag that early.
Can you work with my mediator’s draft?
Yes. We’ll review for court‑required language and align it with filing requirements.
Do you meet clients in person?
Yes, by appointment. We also work fully remote when convenient.
What if English isn’t our first language?
We’ll talk about translation where needed and keep signatures clear so the clerk understands exactly what’s being signed.
