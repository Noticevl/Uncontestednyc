Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?
In most uncontested divorces, no personal appearance is required. We handle e‑filing where accepted and EDDS where directed. If a judge asks for a clarification, we address it in writing whenever possible.
How long does this take?
Many uncontested NYC cases finish 8–16 weeks after both spouses sign. Timing ultimately depends on clerk workloads in your borough and whether any special steps (like international service) are needed.
What exactly does the $5,000 include?
Attorney time from intake to Judgment, standard New York filing fees, and routine NYC personal service. If your matter needs unusual steps—publication, international service, translation—we’ll explain them plainly and show any out‑of‑pocket cost before you choose.
Are there refunds?
Yes. Work is split into three stages. A refund is available before substantive work in Stage 1, before filing/service in Stage 2, and before judgment submissions in Stage 3.
Do both of us have to live in New York?
Not necessarily. New York’s residency statute offers several paths, including one spouse in NY for two years, or one year with ties to NY (e.g., marriage occurred here). We’ll map your facts to a qualifying path at intake.
What counts as “uncontested”?
Both spouses agree to divorce and to all major terms: property, debts, parenting schedule and decision‑making, child support and add‑ons, and any name change.
Can we e‑file?
We use NYSCEF wherever accepted and EDDS where directed. You’ll receive confirmations and a tidy record of everything submitted.
What if we have children?
You’ll need a workable parenting plan and child support terms. We draft in plain English with the clarity clerks expect—holidays, travel, decision‑making, and communication spelled out.
How is child support calculated?
New York’s CSSA applies a percentage to combined parental income and accounts for add‑ons like healthcare, childcare, and extracurriculars. We’ll run the math and include the required worksheets.
Can I keep my home address private?
Yes, you can list a suitable mailing address; the court primarily needs a reliable address for service and notices. We explain options at intake.
Can I restore my former name?
Yes. If requested, the Judgment will restore your former name so you can update records with a single court document.
What if my spouse lives in another state or country?
We handle service by the simplest lawful method, including international conventions if needed. We’ll explain time and cost up front.
What if things become contested mid‑way?
If a dispute arises, we’ll discuss options and any change in scope before work continues. You stay in control.
Is mediation required?
No. Many couples reach agreement directly. If you want a neutral to help finalize terms, we can point you to reputable mediators.
Do I need to be a NYC resident?
No. Several residency paths satisfy the statute even if only one spouse lives in NY or the marriage took place here.
Will a judge ever call me?
It’s rare in uncontested matters. If a judge needs a clarification, it usually arrives as a written note through the clerk.
How do notarizations work?
We use standard notarization and, where permitted, remote online notarization. We’ll confirm what’s acceptable for your documents.
Can we include custom parenting provisions?
Yes—clarity helps everyone. We draft them to be practical and child‑focused, so they pass review.
What about retirement accounts?
If there’s a transfer (e.g., a portion of a 401(k)), we’ll outline whether a QDRO is needed and when it’s prepared.
Do we need financial disclosures?
Uncontested matters generally use simple sworn statements rather than contested‑case discovery. We’ll provide the exact forms you need.
What are the filing fees?
Your plan includes the standard NY filing fees; if a rare outside fee applies (publication, international service), we’ll show it up front before you choose.
Can I sign everything electronically?
Most signatures are accepted electronically; some documents still require ink signatures. We provide plain‑English instructions so nothing is rejected.
What if I need my Judgment fast?
We’ll sequence drafting and filing to avoid back‑and‑forth and give you a realistic range on day one. You’ll get updates at meaningful points.
What if we already separated years ago?
That’s fine. We’ll line up the timeline and make sure your papers reflect your actual separation history.
Will this affect immigration?
Divorce can affect immigration status and sponsorship obligations. If that’s relevant, we’ll flag it and coordinate with an immigration attorney if needed.
