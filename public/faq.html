FAQ

Do I have to appear in court?
Most uncontested matters in NYC are resolved entirely by e‑file/EDDS and mail. Appearance is uncommon; if required, we’ll prepare you and explain why.
How long does this take?
Expect 8–16 weeks after signatures. Timing varies by borough workload and whether your case needs special steps like publication or international service.
What exactly does the $5,000 include?
Attorney time through Judgment, standard filing fees (Index Number and Note‑of‑Issue equivalents), and routine personal service. Two revision rounds. Clear engagement letter.
Are there refunds?
Yes. Stage 1 refund before drafting begins; Stage 2 refund before filing/service; Stage 3 refund before judgment submissions begin.
What counts as ‘uncontested’?
Both spouses agree to divorce and to all terms (property, debt, parenting, support). No one is asking the court to decide disputed issues.
Can we e‑file?
We use NYSCEF when available and EDDS where directed. You’ll receive confirmations and a tidy record of everything submitted.
Do we need a settlement agreement?
If your terms go beyond the standard forms, we’ll prepare a stipulation or agreement that sets out the deal in plain English and satisfies the court’s requirements.
Do both of us have to live in NY?
No. Residency can be met in several ways—for example, one spouse living in NY for two continuous years before filing; or one year with the marriage taking place in NY or the cause arising here.
What if we have children?
Your plan must be workable and specific. We write plainly: where the child lives, parenting schedule, decision‑making, holidays, travel, and communication.
How is child support calculated?
New York’s CSSA formula starts with combined parental income, applies a percentage based on number of children, plus add‑ons. We’ll run the math and include required worksheets.
Can I keep my home address private?
Yes, including using a business address for mail and redacting apartment numbers where appropriate.
Can I restore my former name?
Yes. Include it in the pleadings and Judgment to create a single, permanent record.
What if things become contested mid‑way?
We’ll discuss options. If the case becomes contested, we can refer you to counsel who handle litigation, and we’ll provide your file cleanly.
Is mediation required?
It isn’t required for an uncontested case. If you’re still negotiating, private mediation can be helpful; we can point you to reputable providers.
How do notarizations work?
We use in‑person or remote online notarization (RON) when permitted. You’ll receive instructions with each draft.
Can we customize provisions?
Yes—particularly parenting details, communication norms, and property logistics. We keep terms concrete to reduce clerk questions.
Do we need financial disclosures?
We provide the forms the court expects, and any additional affidavits if requested by a clerk based on your facts.
What are the filing fees?
Standard fees (Index Number, Note‑of‑Issue equivalents) are included in the $5,000 plan. Borough‑specific add‑ons are explained up front if triggered.
Can I sign everything electronically?
We handle e‑sign and e‑file steps where permitted, and provide clear directions where wet ink is required.
What if I need Judgment fast?
We’ll sequence drafting, signatures, and filing quickly. Final timing depends on clerk review queues in your borough.
What if we already separated years ago?
That’s fine. We’ll confirm residency and uncontested terms, then move forward.
Will this affect immigration?
Divorce can intersect with immigration in specific ways. We can refer you to an immigration attorney for tailored advice if needed.
