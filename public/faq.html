Frequently asked questions

Short answers up front; expand for detail.

Do I have to appear in court?

No—most uncontested cases are decided on the papers. If a judge requires a clarification, we handle it and explain what’s needed.

How long does this take?

Many matters complete in 8–16 weeks after both spouses sign. Timing varies by borough workload and completeness of the packet.

What exactly does the $5,000 include?

Attorney time from intake to Judgment, standard NYC filing fees (Index Number and equivalents), routine personal service, two rounds of revisions, and a document archive.

Are there refunds?

Yes. Stage‑based refunds: Stage 1 before drafting starts; Stage 2 before filing occurs; Stage 3 before judgment work begins.

Do both of us have to live in New York?

Not necessarily. NY’s residency statute allows several paths. Example: one spouse living in NY for two years; or one year with NY ties. We’ll map facts to the rule.

What counts as “uncontested”?

Both spouses agree on every material term—property, accounts/debts, parenting if any, support, and name change. If issues remain, we’ll suggest options.

Can we e‑file?

We use NYSCEF wherever accepted and EDDS where directed. You receive confirmations and a clean record of everything submitted.

How is child support calculated?

NY’s CSSA applies percentages to combined parental income, with caps and add‑ons. We provide the math and worksheets with your documents.

Can I keep my home address private?

Yes—use “by appointment” and a mailing address that reaches you. We redact apartment numbers unless a court requires disclosure.

Can I sign electronically?

Where rules allow, we use e‑sign plus notarization solutions; otherwise we coordinate convenient in‑person signing.
