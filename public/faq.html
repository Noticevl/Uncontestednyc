Frequently asked questions

Clear answers for common concerns. Open a question to read the full response.

Do I have to appear in court?

Usually no. Uncontested divorces are mostly paperwork. If the clerk needs clarification, we handle it in writing.

How long does this take?

After signatures, many cases complete in roughly 8–16 weeks depending on the borough and clerk workload.

What exactly does the $5,000 include?

Attorney time to Judgment, standard NYC filing fees, and routine local service. If your case needs unusual steps, we’ll explain options and costs up front.

Are there refunds?

Yes—staged work with refunds available before each stage begins. Stage 1 before drafting work; Stage 2 before filing occurs; Stage 3 before judgment submissions are prepared.

Do both of us have to live in NY?

Not necessarily. New York’s residency statute offers multiple paths. We map your facts to a qualifying path at intake.

Can we e‑file?

Yes, where NYSCEF accepts it. Otherwise, we use EDDS or the required method and give you receipts for everything submitted.

What if we have children?

We’ll add a clear parenting plan and run CSSA support math with required worksheets so review is smooth.

How is child support calculated?

By the CSSA formula with caps and add‑ons. We’ll walk you through the numbers.

Can I keep my home address private?

We can use a business mailing address and redact apartment numbers in public filings when allowed.

Can I restore my former name?

Yes—we can include it in the pleadings and Judgment so it’s permanent and easy to use later.
