Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?
Most uncontested divorces are resolved on papers; in‑person appearances are uncommon. If the court sets a date, we’ll explain why and prepare you.
How long does this take?
A practical range is 8–16 weeks after signatures. Speed depends on the borough’s clerk workload and calendar. We give a realistic range at intake.
What exactly does the $5,000 include?
Attorney time through Judgment, standard NYC filing fees (Index Number and Note of Issue equivalents), and routine personal service. Unusual steps (publication, international service, translation) are quoted up‑front.
Are there refunds?
Yes—stage‑based refunds before each stage starts (Intake/Drafting, Filing/Service, Judgment). Once a stage begins, that stage is earned.
Do both of us have to live in New York?
No. NY’s residency statute has multiple paths. For example, one spouse living in NY for two continuous years; or one year with NY ties. We’ll map your facts to a qualifying path at intake.
What counts as “uncontested”?
You both agree on all issues: property, debt, parenting terms, and support. If anything is disputed, that’s contested and not a fit for this flat fee.
Can we e‑file?
Usually, yes. We use NYSCEF wherever accepted, and EDDS where directed.
How is child support calculated?
Under CSSA with combined parental income, percentages based on number of children, and add‑ons. We’ll run the worksheet and discuss deviations where appropriate.
Can I keep my home address private?
We can use a mailing address and redact sensitive details where permitted. For service addresses and court‑required disclosures, we follow governing rules.
Can I restore my former name?
Yes. We include it in the Judgment so you have a single, permanent document for agencies and accounts.
Do we need a settlement agreement?
If you already have a signed agreement, we’ll review it. If not, the judgment papers can incorporate key terms for an uncontested case.
What if things become contested mid‑way?
We’ll pause, explain options, and costs. If you transition to contested, this flat‑fee plan ends; earned work to date remains earned.
Is mediation required?
Not for an uncontested filing, though many couples reach terms through mediation before hiring counsel.
Will a judge ever call me?
Direct calls are rare. Most communication is via filings and clerk notes.
How do notarizations work?
We support in‑person, e‑notary, or RON where accepted. We’ll arrange what’s practical.
Can we include custom parenting provisions?
Yes—clarity helps. We use concrete language to avoid future disputes.
Do we need financial disclosures?
Uncontested cases still require basic affidavits; we’ll prepare what the court needs and nothing extra.
What are filing fees?
Standard fees are included in the flat fee. If the court or service requires unusual extras, we’ll clear them with you first.
Can I sign everything electronically?
Where permitted, yes. Some forms require wet signatures; we’ll tell you when that applies.
What if I need Judgment fast?
We can sequence your steps to reduce avoidable delays, but the clerk’s queue still governs final timing.
What if we already separated years ago?
That’s fine—uncontested depends on agreement, not recency. We’ll confirm residency path.
Will this affect immigration?
Divorce can affect some processes. We’ll flag where to seek tailored immigration counsel.

Click “Start Your Intake,” complete the form, and we’ll send the engagement letter with next steps.
