Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?
Uncontested divorces are typically paper‑only. We handle clerk questions and status checks so you don’t have to appear.
How long does it take?
Many cases complete 8–16 weeks after signatures. Timing varies by borough workload and judge review practices.
What exactly does the $5,000 include?
Attorney time through Judgment, standard NYC filing fees, routine personal service, and two revision rounds—no surprise add‑ons.
Are there refunds?
Yes. Stage 1 refund before drafting work is done; Stage 2 refund before filing occurs; Stage 3 refund before judgment work begins.
Do both of us have to live in New York?
No. DRL §230 provides several residency paths; we confirm which applies at intake.
What counts as uncontested?
Both spouses agree on all issues and cooperate with signatures and service.
Can we e‑file?
Often. We use NYSCEF where permitted and EDDS where directed. You’ll receive stamped confirmations.
What if we have children?
We include a clear, workable parenting plan and New York’s required child‑support forms.
How is child support calculated?
Under the CSSA using combined parental income and statutory percentages, plus add‑ons.
Can I keep my home address private?
Yes—there are options for confidentiality in sensitive cases; we’ll explain at intake.
Can I restore my former name?
Yes—include it now so your Judgment acts as proof for agencies.
What if my spouse lives in another state or country?
We handle international and out‑of‑state service consistent with NY rules.
Do we need a settlement agreement?
We can include terms within the Judgment package or incorporate an agreement you already signed.
What if things become contested mid‑way?
If a dispute arises we’ll outline options, timelines, and costs before any new work proceeds.
Is mediation required?
No, but it can help if a few terms need discussion. Neutral referrals available.
Do I need to be a NYC resident?
You need to meet one of NY’s residency paths; living in NYC is common but not required.
Will a judge ever call me?
It’s uncommon. If clarification is needed, we handle communications.
How do notarizations work?
We show exactly which pages need a notary and provide mobile/online options.
Can we include custom parenting provisions?
Yes, provided they’re clear and workable for day‑to‑day life.
What about retirement accounts?
We flag when a separate QDRO is needed and explain the next step.
Do we need financial disclosures?
Streamlined financial info is typical for uncontested matters unless a clerk requests more.
Can I sign electronically?
Frequently yes; we’ll specify where wet signatures are still required.
What if I need my Judgment fast?
We file promptly and track closely; some boroughs can move faster than others.
What if we separated years ago?
Still fine if a residency path applies and terms are settled.
Will this affect immigration?
We can coordinate with immigration counsel regarding documents and timing.
