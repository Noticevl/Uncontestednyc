Frequently asked questions

Do I have to appear in court?▾

Not usually. Most uncontested matters are paper‑driven; we file, serve, and track clerk review so you never need to appear in person unless something unusual arises.

How long does it take?▾

Many matters finish 8–16 weeks after you sign, but timing varies by borough workload. You’ll see a realistic range the day we onboard.

What exactly does the $5,000 include?▾

Attorney time from intake to Judgment, standard NY filing fees (Index Number & Note of Issue equivalents), and routine NYC personal service. Anything unusual is explained before you choose it.

Are there refunds?▾

Yes. Refunds are available before work begins in each stage: Stage 1 (before drafting), Stage 2 (before filing/service), Stage 3 (before judgment submissions).

Do both of us have to live in New York?▾

No. New York’s residency statute allows several paths. We map your facts to a qualifying route at intake.

What counts as “uncontested”?▾

You’ve reached agreement on all issues—property/debt division, children, support, and any name change—and no one plans to fight.

Can we e‑file?▾

We e‑file wherever accepted and use EDDS where directed. You’ll receive confirmations and a tidy record.

What if we have children?▾

We ensure your plan is workable: where the child lives, a parenting time schedule, holidays, travel, decision‑making, and communication notes.

How is child support calculated?▾

New York’s CSSA starts with combined income and applies percentages with current caps and add‑ons. We show the math and include the worksheets.

Can I keep my home address private?▾

If safety or privacy is a concern, we can use a designated mailing address and limit publication of apartment numbers where possible.

Can I restore my former name?▾

Yes—include it in the Judgment so you have a single, permanent document to update records.

What if my spouse lives out of state or country?▾

We follow CPLR‑compliant personal service or court‑approved alternatives. International service may require extra steps; we explain costs up front.

Is mediation required?▾

No. Many couples agree on terms through conversation or with a mediator before contacting us. We turn your agreement into court‑ready paperwork.

Do I need to be a NYC resident?▾

No. We file in NYC when venue rules allow; otherwise we’ll point you to the correct county.

Will a judge ever call me?▾

Rarely. Most communication is through clerk notes. We monitor and update you promptly.

How do notarizations work?▾

We support in‑person or remote online notarization (RON) when permitted. You’ll receive simple signing instructions.

Can we include custom parenting provisions?▾

Yes—if they’re clear, workable, and lawful. We help you write them in plain English.

What about retirement accounts?▾

We’ll coordinate any required orders (like QDROs) or tailor your agreement to avoid them where appropriate.

Do we need financial disclosures?▾

Uncontested cases require specific sworn statements; we prepare exactly what the clerk expects—no more, no less.

What are the filing fees?▾

Included in the flat fee: Index Number and final filing equivalents. Non‑standard tasks (publication, translations) are extra and optional.

Can I sign everything electronically?▾

Many documents can be signed electronically with notary options as allowed. We’ll tell you exactly which ones.

What if I need my Judgment fast?▾

We sequence steps to avoid avoidable delay and correct issues quickly. Rush outcomes depend on clerk workload.

What if we already separated years ago?▾

That’s fine—residency and uncontested criteria matter more than the separation date. We’ll align the paperwork with your facts.

Will this affect immigration?▾

We don’t give immigration advice, but we’ll coordinate timing or documentation with your immigration counsel if needed.

How do we start?▾

Click “Start your divorce” or use the quick form. We’ll reply with next steps and a clear timeline.
